At Reliance Internationer Africa, sustainability is embedded in everything we do, from how we source our energy products to how we operate our facilities and support our communities.

Our Sustainability Commitments

Clean Energy Transition

LPG is a proven bridge fuel for Africa's clean energy journey. It produces significantly fewer carbon emissions than coal, firewood and kerosene, the fuels most commonly used across the SADC region for cooking and heating. By supplying affordable, accessible LPG, Reliance Internationer Africa is actively contributing to cleaner air, healthier homes and reduced deforestation across Southern Africa.

We are also actively exploring bio-LPG and renewable gas solutions as the region moves toward net-zero energy goals.

Occupational Safety

The safety of our workforce is non-negotiable. We operate under a zero-harm culture where every employee, contractor and site visitor is fully trained in LPG handling, hazard identification and emergency response protocols.

Regular safety drills, personal protective equipment programmes, and documented risk assessments ensure that our standards remain among the highest in the industry. We believe that safe operations are good operations.

Environmental Responsibility

We take our environmental obligations seriously. All our storage and distribution activities are designed to minimize ecological impact, including spill prevention systems, vapour recovery measures and leak detection protocols at our LPG plant in Lilongwe.

We work alongside local communities and regulatory bodies to ensure our operations do not compromise environmental quality, water sources or biodiversity in the areas where we operate.

Community Wellbeing

Energy access is a fundamental driver of economic development. By supplying clean, affordable LPG to households and businesses across the SADC region, Reliance Internationer Africa is contributing to improved quality of life, reduced indoor air pollution and better health outcomes for millions of people.

We prioritize local employment, building regional capacity and providing training opportunities wherever we operate.

QHSE & ISO 9001 Standards

Our Quality, Health, Safety and Environment (QHSE) management system is aligned with ISO 9001:2000 certification requirements. This means every process, procedure and practice is documented, reviewed and continuously improved to meet internationally recognized standards.

Our management team leads by example, embedding QHSE values into company culture and ensuring accountability at all levels of the organisation.

  • Documented quality management system (ISO 9001)
  • Regular internal and third-party audits
  • Continuous improvement processes across all departments
  • Transparent management review and corrective action systems
